What is a Chit Fund ?

A Chit fund is a kind of savings scheme practiced in India. A chit fund company is a company that manages, conducts, or supervises a chit scheme—as defined in Section of the Chit Funds Act, 1982.
Chit means a transaction which a person enters into an agreement with a specified of persons that every one of them shall subscribe a certain sum of money by way of periodical instalments over a definite period and that each such subscriber shall, in his turn, as determined by lot or by auction or by tender as may be specified in the chit agreement, be entitled to the prize amount.

How does this work?

Following tax issues are involved for a subscriber of chit fund scheme.
There is a new chit group that is commencing. The value of the chit is 500,000. This amount is known as the Chit Value. For the purpose of this illustration, we will consider the duration of the chit to be 25 months. This is known as term period for the chit group.
Chit Fund Company has to gather 25 people who are interested in investing in this chit group. These 25 persons are called Subscribers. Once the chit group commences, the foreman has to register the chit with the registrar of chits. He has to pay 100% of the chit value as security. This amount can be withdrawn only after the said chit group closes and every subscriber is paid what is due to him or her. This regulation protects the interests of the subscriber to a certain extent.
The amount to be saved every month by the Subscriber is Chit value/No of person’s i.e. 5,00,000/25 = 20,000. This is also known as Monthly Subscription. It may be noted here that the number of persons investing in a chit and the duration of the chit is always the same. The maximum monthly contribution or Subscription Amount that a Subscriber will pay is 25,000.
The Subscriber does not always pay the entire Subscription amount every month. Let us find out how and why he does not have to pay the entire amount. The maximum discount or maximum bid permitted ranges from 30 %. For the purpose of this illustration we will assume that a Subscriber has bid 30% of the chit value. 30% of 500,000 is 1,50,000. This amount is known as Chit Discount.
The maximum discount or maximum bid permitted ranges from 30 %. For the purpose of this illustration we will assume that a Subscriber has bid 30% of the chit value. 30% of 500,000 is 1,50,000. This amount is known as Chit Discount. The foreman charges 5% of chit value as commission. In this illustration, that would amount to 25,000. This is deducted from Chit Discount i.e. 1,50,000 -25,000 = 125,000. This amount is available for equal distribution among the subscribers.

In our example, 1,25,000/- would be divided equally among 25 subscribers. Each subscriber would get a discount of 1,25,000/25 = 5000. This amount is known as Dividend. ThIn the following month, all subscribers would have to only pay 15,000 instead of 20,000. This is because they have earned a chit discount of 5000. Depending upon the competition within the chit group the discount percent would vary. The least discount would be 5% which is the foreman’s commission. The bidding process will continue until the end of the chit period.
In this manner, the subscribers can borrow more than what they paid in the first half period of the chit term and save their money if they decide wait further into the chit period. A chit fund creates financial intermediation value by matching depositors' excess cash to borrowers' needs. An efficient matching is done sometimes through auction which results in channeling the money to those in most need for it. The winner of the auction takes away the entire periods collection or part of it and has to repay the amount with interest for the balance period of the chit. The interest rate or the implied form of it is determined by the auction. Typically, the chit fund minimises surplus cash kept with itself so that higher interests are earned.
